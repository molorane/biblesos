[H]Tell Your Friends[/H]
([b]Mark 5:18-20[/b]; [b]John 1:44-51[/b]; [b]Acts 8:25-40[/b])

Everyone who has had an encounter with the Lord Jesus has a testimony that should be shared with those lost in sin. We tend to think that friends and those we know will not listen to us and therefore we choose to witness to those we do not know far from us. This is a false teaching and a deception of the Devil and a trick to those we love.

For many reasons, our relationships with friends and people close to us make our gospel presentation to them easy.
[LI]First, since we have lived and worked with them, our testimonies have meaning and are attractive. They can witness the change that has happened in our lives.[/LI]
[LI]Second, we know them enough that we can choose right ways to approach them and prayer points for their salvation.[/LI]
[LI]Third, the solid issue of coexistence with them gives us an advantage over other believers to reach them.[/LI]
[LI]Fourth, it is a debt we owe to Christ and to everyone to tell them the gospel. We are given a command to tell all creatures the gospel.[/LI]

In preaching the gospel to our acquaintances and relations we should be courteous and polite. They deserve to be respected as we do to those unknown to us. We should not make their evils or known sins our focus of attack. We should not attack their personality either. We have to present Christ and God's plan of redemption and not to condemn. If we testify to them in love and back this up with prayer, many of them will believe and be saved ([b]Matthew 28:18-20[/b], [b]Luke 19:13[/b]).
