Tell Your Friends
(Mark 5:18-20; John 1:44-51; Acts 8:25-40)

Every soul that has had contact  with the Lord Jesus has a testimony to share with those still lost in sin. We sometimes feel
that our friends and those who known us well may not listen to us and as such prefer witnessing to strangers in distant places.
This is a great misconception and deceit of the devil to cheat our loved ones. For a number of reasons, our relationship with
friends and close friends make our relationship easier.

Firstly, having lived or worked with them, our testimonies become relevant and striking. They can bear witness of the change in
 our lives.Secondly, we know them well enough to chose our method of approach and prayer points for our salvation. Thirdly, the
established line of communication also put us at an advantage over other believers in reaching out to them. Fourthly, it is a debt
 we owe to Christ and to all. We have the commission to preach the gospel to every creature.

In preaching the gospel to our acquaintances and relations we should be courteous and polite. They deserve to be respected as we do to
those unknown to us.We should not make their evils or known sins our focus of attack. We should not attack their personality either.
We have to present Christ and God's plan of redemption and not to condemn. If we testify to them in love and back this up with prayer,
many of them will believe and be saved (Matthew 2:18-2;Luke 19:13).
