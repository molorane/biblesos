[H]STEADFASTNESS[/H]
[B]Acts 2:41-42,44,46,47a[/B]

It is not only enough to start on a journey or any venture but what really counts is the manner in which it is continued. Salvation is just the beginning of a journey to the heavenly home. But your eternal salvation depends on your continuity with the Lord.

Steadfastness is keeping firm to one's principles. [LI]It includes continuing in what one has believed ([b] 2 Timothy 3:14[/b]).[/LI]
[LI]It also means continuing in the discipline of life according to the scriptures ([b] 2 Timothy 2:4[/b]).[/LI]
[LI]It means participating in the act of God's work and promoting it ([b] Acts 2:42-47[/b]; [b] 1 Corinthians 15:58[/b]).[/LI]
[LI]It means keeping your relationship with the LORD firm, renewed and growing ([b] 2 Timothy 2:9[/b]).[/LI]
[LI]Also identify with the church ([b] Acts 2:41[/b]; [b] Hebrews 10:25[/b])[/LI]

[S]Benefits of steadfastness in faith[/S]
[LI]1. It helps you to grow ([b] 2 Peter 3:18[/b])[/LI]
[LI]2. It helps you stay standing even in the midst of trials of your faith ([b] Romans 8:35-37[/b]).[/LI]
[LI]3. It makes you bear fruit in the knowledge of our LORD Jesus Christ ([b] 2 Peter 1:8[/b])[/LI]
[LI]4. It makes you become a man or woman that God loves ([b] 2 Timothy 2:21[/b])[/LI]

It's good to be steadfast in faith. Therefore, made a decision. In conclusion, let us reflect on [b] 1 Corinthians 15:58[/b] and pray to God.

[S]Conclusion[/S]: [b] Matthew 24:13[/b]
